Pho Bo (Beef Noodle Soup)

Category: Vietnamese Noodles

Description:

Traditional Vietnamese beef noodle soup with aromatic broth, rice noodles, and fresh herbs. This iconic dish represents the heart of Vietnamese cuisine with its complex flavors and comforting warmth.

Prep Time: 30 min

Cook Time: 2 hours

Serves: 4 people

Difficulty: Medium

For the Broth:
  • 2 lbs beef bones
  • 1 lb beef brisket
  • 1 onion, charred
  • 3-inch piece ginger, charred
  • 2 star anise
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 4 cloves
  • 1 tbsp coriander seeds
  • Fish sauce to taste
  • Salt to taste
For Serving:
Cooking Instructions:
Step 1: Prepare the bones by parboiling them for 5 minutes, then rinse clean.
Step 2: Char the onion and ginger over open flame until fragrant and slightly blackened.
Step 3: Toast the spices (star anise, cinnamon, cloves, coriander) in a dry pan for 2-3 minutes.
Step 4: In a large pot, add bones, brisket, charred onion and ginger, and toasted spices. Cover with water.
Step 5: Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 1.5-2 hours, skimming foam regularly.
Step 6: Season the broth with fish sauce and salt to taste.
Step 7: Cook rice noodles according to package instructions and divide among bowls.
Step 8: Top noodles with sliced beef and ladle hot broth over.
Step 9: Serve immediately with fresh herbs, bean sprouts, lime, and sauces on the side.
Chef's Tips & Notes:

Quality Bones: Use a mix of marrow and knuckle bones for the richest broth.

Charring: Don't skip charring the onion and ginger - it adds essential smoky flavor.

Skimming: Regularly skim the foam during cooking for a clear broth.

Assembly: Have all garnishes ready before serving as pho is best eaten immediately while hot.

Noodles: Fresh rice noodles are preferred, but dried can be substituted.

Japanese Twist: Try adding a small piece of kombu seaweed to the broth for extra umami depth.
